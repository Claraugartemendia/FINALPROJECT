START ADOPTING, STOP BUYING

Why do we have to buy if they are millions of dogs in adoption looking forward to have a person who cares about them?

Many people buy dogs because they may want a specific breed, believe that purchased pets are healthier or better behaved, or simply aren't aware of the number of adoptable animals in need of homes. However, this mindset does not take into account the fact that millions of dogs in shelters are just as capable of providing unconditional love and company.
Adopting a dog not only saves a life but also helps combat the overpopulation crisis. Each year, countless animals in shelters are killed simply because there aren't enough homes for them. By choosing adoption, you give a dog the chance to experience love and care, often for the first time.
Moreover, it is also important to decrease the number of animals in hatcheries that are exploited so that they have more and more pups.
Furthermore, the cost of adoption is also significantly lower than purchasing from breeders or pet stores, and many shelters ensure that adopted pets are already vaccinated or neutered before being adopted.
Adopting is a compassionate choice that aligns with a belief in kindness and sustainability. It's not just about gaining a pet; it's about giving a deserving animal a chance at a better life and sending a powerful message about the importance of rescue over commercial breeding.
